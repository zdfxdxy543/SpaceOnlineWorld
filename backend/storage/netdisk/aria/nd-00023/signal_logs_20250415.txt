CHRONOLOGICAL LOG ENTRIES:

**Entry 1**
Timestamp: 2005-04-14 22:17:03 UTC
Frequency: 1420.405 MHz (Hydrogen Line)
Bandwidth: Extremely narrow (<1 Hz).
Duration: 47 seconds.
Signal Strength: Faint but clear, S/N ratio of 8.4.
Observations: Signal exhibited a non-random, pulsed structure. Pulse intervals followed a repeating pattern of prime numbers (intervals of 2, 3, 5, 7, 11 seconds). Modulation atypical for natural astrophysical sources in this band. No corresponding IFR or civilian transponder traffic logged.

**Entry 2**
Timestamp: 2005-04-15 01:48:22 UTC
Frequency: 327.0 MHz (Deuterium Hyperfine Line)
Bandwidth: Approximately 5 Hz.
Duration: 12 seconds.
Signal Strength: Weak, S/N ratio of 3.1.
Observations: Burst of wide-band noise superimposed on the narrow carrier. Pattern suggests possible data packet 'header' but demodulation attempts yielded no known protocols. Signal cut off abruptly. Cross-reference with seismic monitors showed no local activity.

**Entry 3**
Timestamp: 2005-04-15 04:05:11 UTC
Frequency: Variable, sweeping from 800 MHz to 1.2 GHz.
Bandwidth: Broad (~400 MHz).
Duration: 3 minutes, 18 seconds.
Signal Strength: Moderate and increasing, peaked at S/N 15.1.
Observations: Unauthorized frequency agile transmission. Sweep pattern was logarithmic, not linear. Signal contained embedded harmonic structures that could not be attributed to equipment malfunction. Attempted triangulation failed; source appeared to move or was deliberately obscuring its point of origin. Signal terminated at 04:08:29 UTC.

**Entry 4**
Timestamp: 2005-04-15 05:55:00 UTC
Frequency: 14.2 kHz (Extremely Low Frequency - ELF)
Bandwidth: <0.1 Hz.
Duration: Ongoing (intermittent) as of log compilation.
Signal Strength: Very faint, requires signal averaging to detect.
Observations: Persistent, ultra-low frequency hum. Penetrates geological layers, suggesting a deep or subterranean origin. Pattern is a steady, unmodulated carrier. This is highly anomalous for the local environment and is not consistent with known natural geomagnetic phenomena.
