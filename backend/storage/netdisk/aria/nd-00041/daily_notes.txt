DAILY NOTES ATTACHMENT
Classification: Internal Use Only
Date of Entry: March 14, 2003
Timestamp: 09:45 EST

--- OBSERVATIONS ---
Subject reported a minor disturbance near the eastern perimeter fence at approximately 07:30 this morning. Visual inspection confirmed a section of chain-link had been bent inward, though no breach was detected. Footprints in the damp soil indicated at least two individuals, adult-sized, approaching and retreating from the fence line. No foreign objects or materials were left behind. Security cameras in Sector 4-E were offline for scheduled maintenance from 06:00 to 08:00, coinciding with the reported timeframe. Personnel on duty noted no unauthorized vehicles in the vicinity during routine patrols.

--- ASSESSMENT ---
The incident appears to be a low-level probing action or an attempted trespass that was aborted. The timing suggests possible awareness of the camera maintenance window, though this could be coincidental. The lack of material evidence points toward reconnaissance rather than an active intrusion attempt. Recommend increasing random patrol frequency in the eastern sector for the next 72 hours and reviewing maintenance schedules to minimize concurrent system downtimes. No immediate threat to ongoing operations is assessed, but situational awareness should be heightened.

End of Entry.
