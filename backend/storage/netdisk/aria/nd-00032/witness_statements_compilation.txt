WITNESS STATEMENTS COMPILATION
CASE FILE: 04-2218
INVESTIGATOR: D. Armitage
ARCHIVAL DATE: November 17, 2004

This document is a compilation of initial witness statements pertaining to observed meetings/encounters in the central corridor (sector 4, floors 7-9) during the period of October 28 - November 5, 2004. All statements were taken on November 6, 2004, following preliminary incident report 04-2218-A.

--- STATEMENT 1 ---
WITNESS ID: W-01 (Janitorial staff, assigned Sector 4)
TIMESTAMP OF OBSERVATION: October 31, 2004, approx. 19:45.
STATEMENT: "I was finishing the evening trash collection on the 8th floor. I saw two individuals, a man and a woman, standing by the fire exit stairwell door. They were talking in low voices. The man, late 40s, wore a dark overcoat. The woman had a briefcase. They weren't wearing visitor badges, but I didn't think much of it. It was after hours. The man handed the woman a small envelope. She put it in her coat pocket. They separated, one going into the stairwell, the other towards the elevators. I continued my rounds."

--- STATEMENT 2 ---
WITNESS ID: W-02 (Junior Analyst, Accounting, Office 804)
TIMESTAMP OF OBSERVATION: November 2, 2004, approx. 15:30.
STATEMENT: "I was returning from a late lunch. I passed the same two people near the vending alcove on the 7th floor. They weren't talking this time. The woman was looking at a file folder. The man was looking at his watch. They seemed... focused. I recognized the man from somewhere, maybe a departmental meeting? I nodded, but they didn't acknowledge me. I went to my office."

--- STATEMENT 3 ---
WITNESS ID: W-03 (IT Support Technician)
TIMESTAMP OF OBSERVATION: November 4, 2004, approx. 11:15.
STATEMENT: "I was called to fix a network jack in 9th floor storage. On my way back to the elevator bank, I saw the woman from the previous descriptions. She was alone, standing very still by the potted plant near the north windows. She was holding a mobile phone to her ear but didn't seem to be speaking. She was just listening. She looked tense. After about a minute, she put the phone away and walked briskly towards the stairwell."

--- ASSESSMENT SECTION ---
The three statements, while from different individuals and times, describe consistent actors (one male, one female) engaging in what appears to be coordinated, low-profile meetings in a low-traffic section of the building. The exchange of an envelope (W-01) and the review of documents (W-02) suggest a transfer of physical materials. The solo observation (W-03) involving a silent phone call indicates possible remote coordination or receiving instructions. The corridor's location offers multiple egress points (stairwells, elevators) and minimal CCTV coverage (confirmed with Facilities). The behavior pattern is suspicious and warrants further investigation, including identification of the individuals and cross-referencing with access logs for those dates and times. Recommend escalation to Phase 2 surveillance.

--- END OF COMPILATION ---
