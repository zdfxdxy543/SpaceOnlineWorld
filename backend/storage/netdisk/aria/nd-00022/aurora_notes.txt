PROJECT AURORA PRELIMINARY NOTES
Classification: INTERNAL USE ONLY
Author: Dr. A. Chen, Senior Analyst
Date of Entry: 2004-11-17
Timestamp of Initial Event: 2004-11-15, 03:14:22 UTC

--- OBSERVATIONS ---

03:14:22 UTC: Primary sensor grid (Sector 7-Alpha) registered a significant energy spike. Anomaly registered at 147% above baseline ambient electromagnetic field. Frequency pattern was inconsistent with known terrestrial or scheduled experimental sources.

03:14:25 - 03:15:10 UTC: Fluctuations became rhythmic, exhibiting a damped sinusoidal waveform with a period of approximately 8.7 seconds. Intensity varied between 85% and 155% of baseline. Correlative atmospheric disturbances were noted at the surface monitoring station (light, non-ionized plasma glow reported by night watch).

03:15:30 UTC: Spontaneous termination of the event. All metrics returned to nominal levels. No residual radiation or thermal signatures were detected post-event by standard sweeps.

Subsequent monitoring over the following 48 hours (up to the time of this writing) has shown no repeat occurrences. However, passive listening posts have logged a persistent, very low-level harmonic resonance at 11.3 Hz in the local substrate, which was not present prior to the event.

--- ASSESSMENT ---

The event's origin remains undetermined. The waveform's artificial-seeming regularity and the lack of a clear causative agent (e.g., solar flare, equipment fault) are notable. The correlated visual phenomenon suggests a localized, low-energy atmospheric interaction, possibly a byproduct of the primary fluctuation. The lingering 11.3 Hz resonance is concerning; it may indicate a subtle, persistent alteration of the local field or could be an artifact of sensor calibration post-event. Recommended actions: Isolate and deep-analyze the sensor logs from Sector 7-Alpha for the 60 seconds preceding the spike. Schedule a physical inspection of the sector's hardware. Maintain elevated monitoring status. Further analysis is required before any correlation with theoretical models can be substantiated.
