Suspicious Movement Log - Old Warehouse

Case Reference #: INV-07-2003-WH01
Log Initiated By: Officer K. J. Miller, Surveillance Unit 3
Date Log Commenced: 14 July 2003
Subject Location: Former Allied Machinery Warehouse, 45 Riverfront Drive, South Docks.

---

**TIMELINE OF OBSERVED ACTIVITY**

15 JUL 2003
- 02:17: Two vehicles, a dark-colored van (make/model obscured) and a sedan, arrive at the northeast service entrance. No headlights. Three individuals exit the van, one from the sedan. They appear to carry bulky, medium-sized objects (possible duffel bags or equipment cases) into the warehouse via a side door (Door B). Activity lasts approximately 12 minutes.
- 04:55: Single individual exits Door B, performs a perimeter walk, scanning the adjacent access road. Returns inside after 3 minutes.

16 JUL 2003
- 23:40: Faint, intermittent light observed through upper-floor western windows (previously boarded). Pattern suggests flashlight beams, not fixed lighting. No vehicular activity this night.

17 JUL 2003
- 01:30: The dark van returns. One individual meets it at Door B. Brief exchange of a small package (envelope or box). Van departs immediately. Duration: under 2 minutes.
- Daytime Observation (10:00-16:00): No activity. Property appears deserted. All doors closed. No signage of legitimate business.

18 JUL 2003
- 03:10: Unmarked medium-duty truck (no company logo) arrives. Four individuals begin loading palletized goods, covered in heavy tarpaulin, from the warehouse main roll-door. Loading conducted with notable speed and minimal conversation. Truck departs at 03:38. Warehouse secured.

**OBSERVATIONAL NOTES**
- Subjects exhibit tradecraft awareness: use of light discipline, staggered arrivals/departures, avoidance of main entrance.
- The warehouse was officially condemned and scheduled for demolition in Q4 2002. No utilities are registered as active at the address.
- Local inquiries (discreet) indicate no recent leases or sales filed for the property.
- Sound monitoring (from perimeter) on the night of 17 JUL picked up sporadic mechanical noise, possibly a generator or power tools, inconsistent with simple squatting.

**ASSESSMENT**
The pattern of nocturnal, covert movements involving multiple vehicles and the transfer of concealed goods strongly suggests the warehouse is being used as a temporary staging or storage facility for illicit operations. The level of organization indicates planned activity rather than opportunistic use by vagrants. The loading of palletized materials points to larger-scale logistics. Recommended immediate escalation for closer surveillance and identification of vehicle plates, with a view to determining the nature of the stored materials and the involved parties.

**NEXT REVIEW**
Scheduled for 21 JUL 2003, pending authorization for enhanced monitoring.
