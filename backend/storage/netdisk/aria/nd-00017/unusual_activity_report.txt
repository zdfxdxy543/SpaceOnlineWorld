**WITNESS MEMORANDUM - UNUSUAL ACTIVITY REPORT (COMPILED EVIDENCE)**

**Case ID:** UA-2004-017
**Date of Compilation:** November 12, 2004
**Archivist:** J. Simmons
**Classification:** INTERNAL USE ONLY

**1. SUMMARY OF INCIDENT:**
This document compiles eyewitness accounts and photographic evidence pertaining to multiple reports of unusual activity in the vicinity of the old Ridgewater Industrial Park, specifically around the decommissioned Warehouse #7, between October 28 and November 5, 2004. Reports were initially logged by local patrol officers and forwarded for preliminary investigation.

**2. CHRONOLOGICAL WITNESS ACCOUNTS:**

*   **Witness A (Anonymous Tip, Received 10/28/2004, 23:45):** Caller reported "flashing blue lights, not like police or ambulance, more like a strobe, coming from the upper windows of the warehouse." Caller was driving past on State Route 9. No further contact information provided.

*   **Witness B (Local Resident, Interviewed 10/30/2004, 09:15):** Mrs. Eleanor Vance, residing at 442 Maple Lane (approx. 0.5 miles from site), reported persistent low-frequency humming sounds beginning around 22:00 each night for the past week. Described the sound as "a giant refrigerator, but with a pulse to it." Also noted an increase in stray dog activity and agitation in the neighborhood.

*   **Witness C (Security Guard, Statement taken 11/02/2004, 14:30):** Mr. Frank Dobbins, contracted night watchman for the industrial park, reported finding a perimeter chain-link fence section (south side) "melted and peeled back like tinfoil" on the morning of November 1st. No signs of cutting tools. He observed no trespassers but stated he avoids Warehouse #7 during his rounds due to "a bad feeling."

*   **Witness D (Urban Explorer, Voluntary Statement 11/05/2004, 16:00):** Individual using alias "Moth." Provided two digital photographs (see attached log). Claims to have entered Warehouse #7 on the night of November 4th. Reports encountering a "cold spot" near the central loading bay and witnessing a "shimmer in the air, like heat haze but indoors." Heard metallic scraping sounds from an adjacent room but did not investigate further. Left the premises promptly.

**3. PHOTOGRAPHIC EVIDENCE LOG:**

*   **Photo UA-017-01:** Digital image, timestamp 11/04/2004 20:17. Provided by Witness D. Shows interior of Warehouse #7, main floor. Visible are standard industrial debris and a large, circular pattern of dust disturbance approximately 10 feet in diameter. No identifiable objects in frame.
*   **Photo UA-017-02:** Digital image, timestamp 11/04/2004 20:23. Provided by Witness D. Shows the aforementioned "melted" section of perimeter fencing, corroborating Witness C's account. Fence links appear warped and fused at the midpoint of a 6-foot section.

**4. PRELIMINARY ASSESSMENT:**
The collected accounts, while anecdotal, present a consistent pattern of anomalous events centered on a single location. Key points of convergence include: auditory phenomena (humming), localized thermal anomalies (cold spot), and physical material deformation (fence). The absence of conventional trespass evidence (tool marks, broken locks) is notable. The provided photographs, while low-quality, offer tangible corroboration for the fence damage and suggest unusual activity within the structure. This compilation does not constitute proof of any specific event but provides sufficient supporting material to warrant further, more formal investigation. Recommended next step: Coordinate with county authorities for a daylight site survey and environmental monitoring.

**5. DISPOSITION:**
File forwarded to Editorial and Investigative Desk for background use in developing news feature. Original witness statements and photo files retained in secure evidence locker #4.
