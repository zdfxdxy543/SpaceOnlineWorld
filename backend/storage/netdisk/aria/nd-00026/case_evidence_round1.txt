- Timestamp of Log Generation: 22 July 2004, 09:00 PST (provided by NetOps).

--- PRELIMINARY ASSESSMENT ---
The physical evidence suggests the potential point of initial compromise is local to cubicle 4B. The presence of an untagged USB drive in proximity to the workstation is of high concern, indicating possible use of removable media. The recovered printed queries suggest the individual may have been testing data extraction methods. The firewall logs corroborate suspicious activity originating from this network segment. All items are preserved for detailed forensic examination. Priority is on the forensic imaging of the hard drive from Item 001 and the secure analysis of the USB drive from Item 002. The correlation between the SQL fragments and the target of the outbound connections will be a key investigative thread.

3. Liaise with NetOps to obtain full packet capture data for the target external IP, if available.
4. Cross-reference recovered email addresses with active directory logs for after-hours access.
