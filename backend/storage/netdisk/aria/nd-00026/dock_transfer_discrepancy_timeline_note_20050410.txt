**TIMELINE & EVIDENCE NOTE - DOCKSIDE TRANSFER DISCREPANCY**
**Case Reference:** DT-2025-0410-01
**Date of Compilation:** April 10, 2005
**Compiled By:** Archivist J. Corbin, Internal Records Division
**Subject:** Discrepancy in automated cargo transfer logs for Dock 7, Sector 3, occurring overnight April 9-10, 2005.

**1. TIMELINE OF LOGGED EVENTS (SYSTEM TIMESTAMP GMT -5):**
- **21:47:** Standard system handshake initiated between Dock Control Server (v.2.1.4) and Warehouse 4 inventory management. All green.
- **22:15:** Scheduled nightly maintenance window begins. Logging continues via secondary buffer.
- **23:02:** Primary log file (dockctl_0410_pri.log) records initiation of Transfer Order #7741. Manifest: 12 crates, industrial machine parts, consigned to 'Marlowe & Sons.'
- **23:05:** Forklift RFID scan at Dock 7 Bay B confirms first crate (ID: MP-8892) loaded onto flatbed truck, license plate logged as 'PENDING' due to optical reader fault.
- **23:18:** Transfer Order #7741 marked 'COMPLETED' in primary log. 12/12 crates confirmed via weight sensor and RFID tally.
- **23:30:** Maintenance window ends. System syncs buffers.
- **00:15:** Secondary diagnostic log (dockctl_0410_sec.diag) generated during routine integrity check. Entry at offset 0x2A1F shows a second, unsigned transfer flagged at **23:22**—designated 'ADHOC_XFR'—with no associated order number. Lists 4 crates, commodity type 'UNCLASSIFIED,' destination blank. No RFID verifications logged.
- **03:00:** Automated nightly backup. Discrepancy not flagged by legacy alert routines.

**2. OBSERVATIONS:**
- The 'ADHOC_XFR' entry exists only in the secondary diagnostic log. It is absent from the primary operational log and the warehouse inventory ledger.
- The timestamp (23:22) places it four minutes after the completion of Transfer #7741, during a period of normal system operation post-maintenance.
- No operator login or manual override was recorded in the personnel access log for that console (Station D-7B) between 22:00 and 01:00.
- The truck involved in Transfer #7741 was later identified via street camera footage as a leased vehicle from 'Bayfront Haulage.' Its GPS transponder data for the period 23:00-00:00 shows the vehicle stationary at Dock 7, corroborating the primary log.
- Physical audit of Dock 7 at 08:00 on April 10 found no outstanding crates or irregularities. Warehouse 4 inventory count for machine parts is within expected variance.

**3. INITIAL ASSESSMENT:**
The nature of the secondary log entry suggests a possible data injection or system artifact, rather than a physical transfer of goods. The 'UNCLASSIFIED' commodity flag is atypical for standard shipping protocols and is usually reserved for system-level placeholder data. However, the precise correlation in timing with a legitimate transfer warrants investigation. The absence of an operator signature and the lack of corroboration in primary systems indicate either a sophisticated digital manipulation of the logging subsystem or a previously undocumented software glitch in the diagnostic buffer compiler. Recommended action: Isolate and preserve all relevant log files (primary, secondary, backup) for forensic analysis. Cross-reference with raw sensor feeds from Dock 7 weight scales and perimeter motion detectors for the 15-minute window centered on 23:22.
