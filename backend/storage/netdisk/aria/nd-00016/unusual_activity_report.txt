**UNUSUAL ACTIVITY REPORT**
**Case ID:** UAR-2003-117
**Date Filed:** October 24, 2003
**Author:** Sgt. J. Miller, Nightwatch Coordinator
**Status:** OPEN / UNDER REVIEW

**1. SUMMARY OF REPORTED INCIDENTS:**
This report consolidates civilian and patrol officer observations of anomalous events within the town limits, recorded between October 17 and October 23, 2003. The activity does not fit established patterns for vandalism, animal disturbance, or meteorological phenomena. All incidents occurred between 11:00 PM and 4:00 AM.

**2. DETAILED OBSERVATIONS:**
- **17 OCT 2003, ~23:45:** Resident at 34 Elm Street reports a "low, rhythmic humming" audible for approximately ten minutes, accompanied by brief, localized power flickering. No visible source. Patrol unit dispatched; no findings.
- **19 OCT 2003, 01:30:** Officer Daniels, on routine patrol near the old water tower, observes a "cluster of faint, bluish lights" drifting slowly above the tree line in the undevelopated woods west of Ridge Road. Lights vanished upon attempted approach. No aircraft or weather balloon activity reported by regional FAA that night.
- **21 OCT 2003, 03:15:** Multiple calls from the downtown area regarding all streetlights on Main Street between 2nd and 5th Avenue simultaneously dimming to 10% brightness for a period of 90 seconds before restoring. Power company logs show no grid fluctuation or fault.
- **22 OCT 2003, 02:00:** Security camera (grayscale, low-res) from the closed-down 'Gas-N-Go' on Route 9 captures a tall, indistinct figure moving across the parking lot. Figure appears to move from the center of the lot to the tree line in 3 frames (approx. 3 seconds), a distance of over 50 feet. No subsequent footprints or physical trace found.

**3. ASSESSMENT:**
While individually each incident could be dismissed as technical glitch, wildlife, or prank, the temporal clustering, consistent nocturnal timing, and lack of physical evidence or responsible parties is notable. The events share no direct geographical overlap but are all within a 2-mile radius of the town center. No injuries or property damage reported. Recommended action: Continue logging all related reports, increase sporadic late-night patrol visibility in reported areas, and await further developments. Possible coordination with county emergency management advised if pattern escalates.

**END REPORT.**
