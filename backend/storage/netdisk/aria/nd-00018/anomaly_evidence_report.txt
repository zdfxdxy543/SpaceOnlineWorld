**INCIDENT REPORT: EVIDENCE OF UNUSUAL ENERGY FLUCTUATIONS**
**Report ID:** EFR-2004-089
**Date Filed:** November 17, 2004
**Reporting Officer:** Archivist J. Varma
**Classification:** INTERNAL USE ONLY

**1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY**
This report documents persistent anomalous energy signatures detected within the station's primary power distribution network (Grid Sigma) between November 10 and November 15, 2004. Fluctuations deviate significantly from baseline parameters, exhibiting non-standard waveforms and localized voltage spikes without identifiable source or load correlation. Initial diagnostics have ruled out standard equipment failure. The anomalies appear to be increasing in amplitude and duration.

**2. CHRONOLOGICAL OBSERVATIONS**

- **2004-11-10, 03:14 UTC:** First registered deviation. Monitoring Station Delta logged a 0.5% variance in AC frequency on Sub-grid 4-C. Lasted 4.2 seconds. No alarms triggered; logged as a minor transient.

- **2004-11-12, 22:47 UTC:** Sustained fluctuation event. Primary transformer hub Tango-7 reported a series of voltage spikes, peaking at 127% of nominal (304V on a 240V line). Event duration: 18 seconds. Automated safeties engaged, causing a brief brownout in Sector G. Physical inspection found no fault in transformer hardware.

- **2004-11-14, 11:03 UTC:** Anomalous waveform captured. Oscilloscope data from Junction Node 42 shows a superimposed high-frequency signal (approx. 2 kHz) on the standard 50 Hz sine wave. Signal exhibited a non-harmonic, quasi-random modulation pattern. Lasted for 9 minutes, 11 seconds. Accompanying magnetic field distortions were noted by nearby lab personnel (see attached statement from Dr. Aris).

- **2004-11-15, 07:30 UTC:** Most significant event to date. Grid-wide monitoring recorded simultaneous fluctuations across three independent sub-grids. Voltage and current readings became desynchronized for a period of 31 seconds, indicating a potential phase reality distortion. Emergency protocols were initiated but stabilized before full isolation was required. Residual thermal signatures were detected at several conduit junctions, despite no increase in electrical resistance.

**3. EVIDENCE COLLECTED**
- Digital oscilloscope logs from Nodes 42, 17, and 89.
- Raw data streams from the Grid Sigma SCADA system for the relevant period.
- Hard copies of alarm logs (annotated).
- Three (3) ambient electromagnetic field recordings from affected sectors.
- Witness statement from Dr. L. Aris, Project Gamma lead, regarding observable "flickering" in containment fields concurrent with the 11:03 event.

**4. PRELIMINARY ASSESSMENT**
The anomalies are not consistent with known engineering failures, load imbalances, or external grid interference. The coordinated nature of the November 15 event suggests a non-localized source or an emergent property of the grid itself. The high-frequency signals bear superficial resemblance to theoretical resonance patterns associated with dimensional bleed-through, but this is highly speculative. Immediate concerns are for equipment integrity and personnel safety. A dedicated investigative task force is recommended to correlate this data with any concurrent experimental activity or environmental sensor readings from the same timeframe.
